The Year Thus Far
2018 has not gone well for the overall market up until this point, this should be no surprise for anyone. The sharp correction that greeted us at the beginning of the year was not limited to just cryptocurrencies but rather the effects were observed in traditional global markets as well. There were several key variables which became more concerning with each uptick in movement. A few examples of those 2018 variables
Looking Forward
Although the price is still off by more than 50% from the December high, I see very positive and significant signs of a new marketplace being created. This marketplace will house our current traditional sectors (Financial Services, P2P, Supply Chain, Manufacturing, Health Care, Banking, etc.) as well as whole new sectors being created (AI computing rentals, Smart Contracts Applications, Decentralized File Storages ( (IPFS), decentralized crowd funding, Banking services for the Unbanked, decentralized education, etc.). Visualizing the above statement may be difficult, however, think of an Amazon type marketplace where retail and institutional customers can access to get an Uber, order Seamless, purchase their insurance policy, secure a line of credit, or access an infinite number of other applications to interact with.

The developments in this field are running at such a fast pace that even the most attentive spectator is likely to miss a good deal of the action. There is much more going on in this sector than the call for regulations on one hand and the surge of illegal profiteers on the other. The new boom has made room for many promising projects of the second and third blockchain generation. The blockchain has not only made it into the mainstream but it is also slowly seeping into daily life as more and more existing companies are taking a closer look at blockchain technology.
2018 FUD Variables
International Regulatory Standards / Discussions Sharp Correction causing Feasibility of Asset Class Negative / Fake News Influential Persons Denouncing Cryptos Media Airtime (High / Low depending market cycle) “Scam Coins” “Exchange Hacks”
Is there Hope?

Luckily, as time passed several of those variables have been clarified, answered, or have implemented workarounds. As a result, the markets determined the low of ~6k BTC at the end of March was the bottom and signaled the reversal of the downtrend to begin its climb back up to more favorable levels.

    Market Summary

    The month of May opened with a market cap of 423 billion and effectively traded sideways for the duration of the month with the MC closing at 343 billion. The much anticipated post Consensus crypto rally did not materialize. Instead, heading into the closing stages of May, market participants really took to heart the old saying “sell in May and go away”. The sentiment remained cautious, as regulators on both sides of the pond upped the ante in their fight to clean up the industry.

    Tulsi

    Similar to April, we contiune to build reserves by removing risk (selling off portions of positions) when the market price deems it feasible from Tulsi's perspective. We contiune to hold positions and limit trading to minimal amounts in regards to entering new positions at this time. Volume is picking up with prices also ascending from a Marco point of view. At this time, we are confident in the positions held and we are waiting for further milestones from those respected projects.
